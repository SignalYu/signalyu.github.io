2024-12-03: Stress, Worry, Anxiety, Depression#

Stress refers to the body’s physical or psychological response to external presures or demands. Worry is a mental process where a person fixates on potential problems or negative outcome in the future. Anxiety is a feeling of unease, nervousness, or fear, often related to an imminent event or uncertain outcome. Depression is a mood disorder marked by persistent feelings of sadness, a lack of interest in previously enjoyable activities, and a diminished sense of energy and motivation.

2024-12-04: Facts, Truth, Opinion, Stance#

Facts are objective statements that can be verified and proven true or false through evidence or observation. They are independent of personal fellings or beliefs. For example, “Water boils at 100°C at sea level.” is a fact.

Truth refers to what is in accordance with reality or fact. It may vary in different contexts, such as moral, scientific, or personal truth. For example, “Honesty is a virtue.” is a truth.

Opinion is a subjective belief, feeling, or assessment about something, which is not universally verifiable and is influenced by personal experiences, perferences, or biases. For example, “I think this movie is boring,” or “Chocolate is the best ice cream flavor” are opinions.

Stance is a position or attitude toward a particular issue, often shaped by a combination of beliefs, opinions, and facts. A stance can be publicly declared, indicating support or opposition to something. For example, “I support stricter environmental regulations.” is a stance.

In summary: Facts serve as the foundation for truth. Opinions are shaped by facts but often include personal beliefs and feelings. Stance is a stronger form of opinion.

Real-World Example: Climate Change

  1. Fact: The Earth’s average temperature has increased by approximately 1.2°C since the late 19th century.
  2. Truth: Climate change is a serious issue that is affecting ecosystems and human societies.
  3. Opinion: I think the government should take immediate action to combat climate change, even if it means stricter regulations on industries.
  4. Stance: I am an advocate for policies that reduce carbon emissions and increase investment in renewable energy sources to combat climate change.
